ART THOUGHTZ

2010, color, sound, HD video

“Art Thoughtz” is a web video series created by Jayson Musson between 2010 and 2012 where he adopts the alter ego Hennessy Youngman, a humorous art critic who speaks with a mix of confidence and satire. Through Hennessy’s comedic “how-to” tutorials like How to Be a Successful Artist and Relational Aesthetics, Musson exposes the racial, class, and institutional biases of the contemporary art world while parodying both academic art language and hip-hop culture. In one memorable episode, he jokes that “relational aesthetics” is just when someone with an MFA wants to meet new people but doesn’t know how to talk to them normally. The series became a viral and influential commentary on how digital culture, identity, and art criticism intersect, encouraging viewers to laugh while also reflecting on what it means to belong in the art world.
